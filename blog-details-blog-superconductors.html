Superconductors: The Next Chapter of Technologies

  • May 2nd 2017
  • Posted By: Carrot Press
  • 14 Minute Read

Superconductors are emerging into the technology world immensely strong throughout the last decade, and with the discovery of new superconductors; possibly manageable superconductors, it is safe to say that the idea of using superconductors in more technological advancements in the near future is a rational notion.



What is a Superconductor?



Superconductors are either very cold or very hot, very cold material, oftentimes a metal, that allows electricity to pass through it thus, categorizing it as a superconductor. Can you think of a superconductor that we have already discovered, which is primarily in a liquid form and metallic?






The Discovery of the First Superconductor



If you guessed Mercury, you’re correct: 100 super-points to you. Mercury is the first superconductor we discovered on this beautiful blue dot, and the element has established itself to be one of the most useful elements for technological devices from cell phone batteries; alongside medicinal and data collection devices like barometers and thermometers. Mercury possesses a chemical characteristic that allows it to be an extremely accurate reactor to changing temperatures.



Advancements with Superconductors



If utilized correctly, superconductors can be a primetime element for the advancement of humankind. Some include advancements in transportation, computing and energy conservation. With this innovative phenomena of technology newly-discovered by humans, especially extremely cold materials that are superconductive, scientists are building their knowledge on how to create a warmer, more manageable superconductor for various purposes.



Transportation




Japan's Fastest Train - the Maglev: Uses a series of magnets to levitate and propel the train forward.

One of the most fascinating roles superconductors can play in the future of human lives is its impact on the way we travel. In a recent experiment, scientists have discovered the Meissner effect, where a superconductive object, if it is to it’s critical low temperature, can completely repel the magnetic fields, and attain virtually no resistance within an electrical current; resulting in a levitating object. Engineers are now in the works of creating levitating train systems, with the ‘tracks’ made out of magnets to create a magnetic field for the superconducting train.



So how is the Meissner effect going to affect the way we travel? Well, if biofuels and alternatives didn’t exist and we happened to go through an inevitable crisis of running dangerously low on petroleum, we could completely get rid of using gas for transportation all together with superconductor transportation.



When can we expect the technological advancements in transportation going to start emerging into our everyday lives? And the answer to that is sadly not quite yet (or at least in the next decade) considering scientists and researchers are working vigorously to safely handle a superconductor, as the ones that can levitate over a magnetic field, could also fuse your hand to the metal, it’s that cold.





So, you could see how big-house car companies would respond to that if they were proposed to a new technology like that - they would simply turn it down for too many liabilities. The eagerness to learn more about these superconductors and how they can be handled and used in our day-to-day lives with more relevance is a very real thing.



Quantum Computing



Quantum computing with superconductors is a very real vision of the future - supercomputers that are 100x more powerful than the ones we have today. Today, large tech companies like Google, IBM, Intel BBN and IMEC Technologies have already undergone the research and even prototype construction of several quantum computers.




One of five Google quantum computers at a lab near Santa Barbara, California.

Stephen Shankland/CNET

In the near future, as soon as the next decade, we could see supercomputers in the use of everyday life with the onset of robotics. The possible missing component to a super computer could be the use of quantum crystals, which is an extraterrestrial material, proving difficult to obtain and gather better knowledge of what they are and how they work.



Energy Conservation



With the onset of quantum travel and quantum computing, life would be completely different than it is today - as we are on the verge of these advancements. The way we attain and use energies such as petroleum fuels would be a thing of the past. With such scientific advancements in levitation as described above, the cost of getting people from place to place in particularly a small amount of time, at higher speeds and longer distances is not out of the question.



The storage of electrical energy using a superconductor is much more efficient than that of modern energy storage considering the electrical energy made and stored by a closed superconductor loop can last months as opposed to the 2-3 days duration of a typical power station that is shut off, and much more efficient than a hydroelectric plant; releasing more water when demand is high.



With all that said, there have been some installations of high-temperature room conductors for the transmission of electrical energy (instead of the use of copper or aluminum wire cables). However, these installations of superconductors, because they are at very high-temperatures, lose some of their energy through its resistance. Scientists propose that if we were to discover and use a room temperature superconductor in substitute for the high-temperature material for cables or wires, we could cut that energy loss in half, and virtually have no resistance (or energy loss) with this particular method.



What We Could See for Superconductors in the Future



Superconductors, in all honesty, have no bounds for the future - we could virtually see our whole world changed by this phenomenon. The way we move and use our energies could be assisted by the new discovery of room-temperature or manageable superconductors that could advance our society immensely. Complex extraterrestrial lifeforms utilize this technology and frankly, are more advanced than we are so it is our destiny that we harness this technology and take full grasp of superconductors.
