Architectural Home Trends of 2015

  • October 19th 2015
  • Posted By: Carrot Press
  • 13 Minute Read

In this collection of architectural trends of 2015, we take a look at some modern designs that simply surpass the imagination - from little homes, to treehouses, this year will certainly prove as a splash in variety of styles in regards to the new designs for the structures we live, work and commute with. Let’s dive in to this year’s architectural home trends.


Geometrical Homes - Trendy or Trippy?



Known as the Valley House, it’s nestled in valley of the Dolomite Mountains, Italy

Sarah Barnes, MyModernMet

If you wonder if you’re seeing a home from something out of the movie, Toys, then you’re probably right. Asymmetry - yes, every OCD person’s nightmare. But, will it be a trend that catches on in this new modern age of architecture that we witness throughout each year? It could quite possibly do so, just take a gander at this concept cabin design made with diamond shapes as an inspiration for angles and lines.


Interior, master bedroom of the Valley House

Sarah Barnes, MyModernMet

Little Homes: The Craze is On


Little home in Springfield, MO at a repurposed mobile home lot

Brenna Stark/Springfield News-Leader

If you thought everyone liked big stuff in the ‘McMansion’-loving country of America, think again - we love to go little, too! Well, except for you, Texans (we’re pretty sure size matters there and it better be big, whatever it is).


If the little home is pre-fabricated, it’s even in more of a demand for sales of 2015 into 2016 - the pre-builts are selling like hotcakes to savvy homeowners looking to downsize in a big way.


Let’s admit it, we consciously live to consume, but we subconsciously want to emit a smaller footprint doing so. Little homes are an essential solution for this concern millions of Americans are now having, with the oil crisis of 2009, several hundreds of thousands are considering a smaller home.


Photo by Wheelhaus

Others, just love small. Small is easy to manage, easy to clean, doesn’t take much to design and can become a luxurious mobile home on wheels if need be. A cutesy ‘pocketbook’ trend, we will see little homes stay in the mix for at least a decade to come.


Mancaves, ‘Hoorah!’



A fortified mancave in Los Angeles, design by Atlas Survival Shelters.

Photo/Homecrux

Man Caves are in! Yes, we said it guys - and hard as hell to believe, us dudes are all in when it comes to putting on our interior designer hats (getting in touch with that feminine side) to put together a clean, but masculine space for that much needed guy time. In 2015, bonus rooms are now turning into guy-inspired spaces.


One creative design we have seen in Los Angeles, the fortified zombie shelter, complete with galvanized corrugated pipes as defense walls, it’s the perfect getaway to keep you cozy and safe from any fallout or disaster.


Other man cave concepts trending this year are the sports bar or theater themes. Man caves are the perfect area to showcase sports memorabilia, trophies and awards or an ample space to get together with friends and watch a movie or football game.


Trophy room and home theater-themed mancave.



External Ventilation Pipes/Lighting




A trend that has stood the test of time and emerging as a revamped trend of the early 1900’s, external ventilation pipes and lighting are coming back strong. As seen primarily in art studios, coffee shops and workplaces, the showcase of external lights and pipes inside a home is a sophisticated touch to the bland, every day covered ceiling.


Surprisingly, exposing the ‘guts’ of an area is a sight for sore eyes, bringing attention to the good bones of a building, and emphasizing its beauty.



Electrical and Ventilation systems exposed in an office.

Photo: Remodelista

Meshing Contemporary and Rustic


Rustic-contemporary home in Lake Tahoe, California

Photo Credit: Sagemodern

Close to the hearts of many, it’s the beauty of rustic flare with the sophistication of contemporary design married together to create a fresh new trend. In this style, we see reclaimed wood backsplashes with the use of straight cut rock and minimalistic layouts to pave a new style for home architecture.


Subtle angles, large columns and straight lines characterize a rustic-contemporary and, with a keen eye, you can identify elements of industrial design dotted within the architecture.


Interior offers an array of straight, subtle angles with rustic touches

Photo Credit: Sagemodern

The coined style emphasizes the beauty of nature, giving way for a blend that is harmonious between man, shelter and the surroundings.


Nature blends seamlessly with pine wood accents in the master bedroom

Photo Credit: Sagemodern

Whimsical Organic Homes



Known as the Snow White Cottage, it sold for over three quarters of a million



2015 is the year for storybook style fun, and the Snow White style has crawled it’s way onto the trend wave. As more homeowners are designing organically shaped homes, they are noticing more intimacy with the materials that make up the shelter that protects them from the elements - gaining a sort of appreciation for it. We can see these organic styles used predominantly in tree houses, playhouses and homes around the world from South America to Peru. If we gave you a pile of sticks, could you create something to live in and be proud of? Now you can see why these structures are so praised, they are difficult to make! Let’s give a hand to imagination, shall we?


Orb playhouse located in a backyard, not for rough play
