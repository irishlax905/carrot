Gold Reaches Highest Value in Decades - How To Scrap Gold from Electronics

  • July 2nd 2020
  • By Carrot Press
  • 17 Minute Read

If you choose to utilize or perform upon any of the following information, do so at your own risk. Carrot does not guarantee the results claimed within this article as results may vary.



Gold has reached its highest value since September 2011, almost a decade ago, and is predicted to surpass a whopping $2,000 per ounce, come August 2020. So, how do you get your hands on this precious commodity that is determined to only rise in value as years pass?


Well, for those of you with a weak back, no desire to hunt or dig a hole for gold (we don’t blame you, it’s not for the faint of heart), or any other underlying reason to turn down prospecting outdoors, there is always the art of scrapping. Scrapping, that is to say, is not an easy task to achieve a reputable amount of gold from, without the right tools and know-how to be able to make a good payout from your efforts.



What You Need to Scrap


1. Computer Scrap (the more, the merrier)


ifong via Shutterstock

Make sure that your computer scrap contains circuit boards, memory cards, RAM cards and any other components that provide circuitry, power or energy of some sort to the device you’re scrapping.


Important Note: Gold is conductive - it is used in gadgets to conduct electricity proficiently; so stick away from frames, plastics parts, chorded components, etc. for scrapping.

2. Micro-Screwdrivers and Computer Tools



Sprotek 58 Piece Computer Repair Tool Kit Purchase Here


Important Note: To take the computer scrap apart, you’ll need a series of star bits, micro flathead screwdrivers, and any other tools needed to break down the parts. Don’t be afraid to rip a few things apart here, the gold you’re after is pretty thin, and cannot be damaged from taking apart the equipment.

3. Beaker or Mason Glass Jar



These are strictly used to collect and separate all your gold-bearing computer scrap. Use one jar or beaker to collect all your pieces that you’ve seen gold on, or use several to separate piles into ones that you’re sure have gold in them, and ones that you’re still working on. Each scrapper has their own method, and like Reese’s, there’s no wrong way to collect the gold.


4. Plastic Colander



Get your plastic colander here


Used for straining the unwanted materials away from the gold when the gold is stripped and loosely moving away from the unwanted materials. You'll need a plastic one so the acid doesn't eat through it.


5. Water


We’ll use plenty of water to wash away all the chemicals from the gold and unwanted materials once everything is stripped, and loosely moving independently.


6. Muriatic (Hydrochloric) Acid



Although you may get some weird looks from the cashier for purchasing muriatic acid from your local hardware store, ensure to them that you’re not disintegrating dead bodies in your backyard and walk out the store casually. You’ll need this stuff to literally eat away all the extra metals that are surrounding the gold. It will also share a dual purpose of stripping away the gold from the plastics or metals it may be adhered to, such as RAM chips.


7. Crucible and Tongs



You can get your ceramic crucible and tongs here


Yay, we get to be forgers of gold, like an episode of Witcher. You have the choice of journeying through the Mead Creek Mire to talk to old Wizard Eiker about acquiring a crucible from his dusty inventories, or you can be a regular human being and buy your crucible offline.


8. Plastic Bucket



A 5-gallon bucket is used to collect the unwanted chemicals and scrap from your collection. You can pick up a plastic 5-gallon bucket at your nearest hardware store.


9. Blow Torch/Forging Furnace



Now, the tool to heat the crucible hot enough to melt the metal. You can either use a mixed (MAPP) gas torch (we recommended mixed gas torch because it burns hotter than a regular butane torch, look for the yellow BernzoMatic canister), or a forging furnace, if you’re somewhat of an elitist - it’s utterly and completely up to you. We just ask you to please make sure to stay safe and wear protective gear like fire gloves and safety goggles, while handling a blow torch and crucible.


10. Coffee Filter


Available at any grocery store, we’ll use a coffee filter to catch the fine gold and flakes from the water it will be contained in.


11. Tweezers


We'll use these to handle the hot gold in the crucible!


12. Fire/Chem Safety Gear (Goggles, Gloves)



Get your safety gear here


13. Soup Bowl of Cold Water


Our final item, used for cooling your nugget of gold once it’s scrapped and smelted. Let’s get started, shall we?


Step One: Start Scrapping


Step one is quite possibly the most daunting and time-consuming part of scrapping, as it consists of breaking the computer parts down to a smaller piece that contains the gold. There is a good chance you will lose pieces that contain gold in this step. That is okay, it just depends on how much scrap you have to go through, and how much time you’d like to put into finding all of it.

Once you’re finished collecting all the relatively small pieces containing gold, you’ll want to collect it into your jar or beaker.


Important Note: Sometimes the gold is hard to identify within the small components of electronics - it is not as shiny and lustrous as raw gold found within a creek or river. Keep a keen eye out for a gold-colored shine of any sort and do not be fooled of the darker-colored, copper.

Step Two: Melt it Off


IMPORTANT: ALWAYS USE GLOVES, MASKS AND GOGGLES HANDLING HYDROCHLORIC (MURIATIC ACID)



Credit: sreetips/YouTube

Now it’s time to take your muriatic acid and burn away all the unwanted materials and essentially strip the gold from what it’s embedded to. Grab your glass jar or beaker full of gold bearing material and pour enough hydrochloric acid (or muriatic acid) into the jar or beaker, making sure to submerge all the material. Let the material soak in the acid for about 14-24 hours. Stir the jar of material every 3-5 hours, ensuring that you strip away all the gold.. You may notice a shimmer or sparkle when you stir, that’s the gold saying hello to you!



Credit: sreetips/YouTube

Important Note: The liquid will turn a dark color - first green (from the copper dissolving first, and then a darker green from all the other metals reacting with the chemical. You can use hydrogen peroxide to create an AP solution, to speed up the stripping process.

Step Three: Rinse and Strain


Time to pour the concentration and loose material into a strainer. Set your colander over the top edges of your plastic bucket, and carefully pour the jar of material and acid into the strainer and bucket.



Rinsing the scraps under a faucet with the strainer above the plastic bucket.



Gracefully rinse the loose gold flakes away from the unwanted material, through the colander and to the bottom of the bucket. Discard the bare, gold-less material and continue to wash and strain the gold flakes to the bottom of the bucket.


COLLECT THE DILUTED CHEMICALS INTO A DESIGNATED PLASTIC OR GLASS CONTAINER, COVER IT WITH A LID AND LABEL IT ‘BIOHAZARD’ AND DISPOSE OF IT PROPERLY AT YOUR LOCAL DUMP. WE CANNOT STRESS THIS ENOUGH, BE GOOD TO OUR PLANET WHILE YOU MAKE MONEY AND DISPOSE OF HAZARDOUS CHEMICALS CORRECTLY.


After you have clear (mostly water) and gold scrap inside your bucket, you’ll place your coffee filter into your plastic colander and strain the liquid and gold flake once more. Patiently strain all of the liquid through the filter to leave an allustrious pile of gold within the strainer. Wait until the filter is completely dry and scrape the gold away from the strainer into your crucible with one of your computer tools, a flathead works great.


Step Four: Smelt and Cool the Gold


Now it’s time to take that fine, flakey gold and turn it into a beautiful little nugget, or picker as the ol’ timers used to say. The way we’re going to achieve that is by smelting the gold you’ve collected.

After you’ve collected all your dried gold flake into the crucible, gear up with all your fire safety gear, and equip your blow torch.


You’ll need to heat the crucible up for some time, until it’s basically red-hot.



Important Note: A good way to tell if your crucible is to temperature to melt gold is to turn off the lights, you’ll be able to notice the glow of the crucible more efficiently this way.

Once your nugget is melted into a ball, and you notice this through the glow of the crucible, you’ll want to grab your needle-nose tongs, not your crucible ones you’re hopefully holding, and grasp the nugget of gold firmly and transfer it into the bowl of cold water. Once it’s cool to the touch, add it to your collection or post it on social media to show your friends all the hard work you put in to get it!
