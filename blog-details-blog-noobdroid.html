3 Mistakes Made by Android Developer Noobs

  • Jun. 18 2020
  • Posted By: Carrot Press
  • App Development

With the Android app store growing over 10x more of any other app store as of 2019, 2020 brings a new wave of savvy Android developers that experience common mistakes while developing for the platform. Some of which, we hope to cover in this short but impertinent list of common mistakes made by Android developer newbies.



1. Merging from iOS



Android and Apple seem to have not played nicely together in the past decade of app publishment; as these two fierce competitors have butted heads ever since then however, in recent years, they have taken steps to take care of this first mistake that beginners make - the switch from iOS to Android Studio - as mentioned, the process has become more smooth over the years with less problems but you still can find a ‘cloned’ app or two in the Android app store if you look closely enough, which will never function or look quite right in the Android store as it is.



The other side of the mistakes we’ve seen being made is the format switch from Swift, or (hopefully not) Objective-C, to Android-friendly formats. Granted, Swift can manifest in an Android app but again, it’s not ideal to use this method of switching over. Keep in mind, the formats needed for backend operations, as well.



We’ve listed a few examples of how merging to Android can cause these errors:



  • 1. Stick to a logo title screen for only the beginning of your app as Android cannot suffice for random splash storyboard protocols.
  • 2. Keep a list of what formats you’re using in each UI.
  • 3. Keep in mind the app icon is a different dimension and shape for Google Play apps.
  • 4. Standalone tabs don’t work on Google phones like iOS phones do.


2. Get That Code Right



Always keep in mind the type of Android app you want to make, and what code or frameworks it will need to complete or achieve your mobile app goals. Is your app complex? Does it require backend operations? How many servers will it need if any? Is it a social or utility style app? Many questions are needed to be answered in order to undergo a development phase for Android apps.

Also unlike iOS, the phone screen size is something to consider, as iOS devices are relatively the same size and, Apple always makes sure their developers have sufficient UI dimensions for each of the devices that are functioning in the real world. As Android umbrellas a vast array of devices and screen sizes, it is critical to test as many devices with trusted online simulators or software simulators to ensure you are developing a universally-friendly app experience. Now rotating those devices? Well that’s a whole different battlefield.



3. Much Too Many





Even though the Android app store has a vast array of apps; everything under the sun for what you would need for your day-to-day life, you will still find that there are plenty of duplicates for each category, even niche categories.



And as we’ve seen, Android has no issue denying apps that are duplicates of other apps or similar to their experience, and are now looking for more unique app concepts, following Apple’s lead with this policy. That’s why it's more important than ever these days to develop your concept, and can be the most critical step of your whole production. Furthermore, a unique app is a successful app - bound to give you more downloads than an app that is the same as another.
