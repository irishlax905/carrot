4 Things to Check Off When Launching a Laravel Website

  • Nov. 30 2015
  • Posted By: Carrot Press
  • Web Development

We all know Laravel is a powerful framework for both websites large and small, but do you attain a checklist of important aspects to make sure of before launch? We’ve put together a micro-list of 5 very crucial tasks to go over before you set yourself up for web development failure without a checklist. Keep in mind, this is a subjective collection of tasks for each project - perhaps you’ve already checked these factors off or maybe the checklist does not apply to the sort of installations you are working with. So without further delay, here we go:



4. Backup, Backup, BACKUP!



Even though we ranked backups at 4 in this countdown list, it is simply put, just as vital as the first one. Essentially, we ranked it at number 4 so you can read it first, think about it first, and more prominently, think about it all the time when your project is live. Why is this? Because data leaks, and lossage is the single worst nightmare any developer can go through. You wouldn’t want to experience this, so we always say backup and, always and make sure your database is configured to do so.



3. Test That Stuff, Man!



Testing the features of your websites almost seems obvious to all of us however, many developers rush through this phase. Testing all aspects of your website including autonomous environments and backend operations are all important tasks to complete. Users will find ways to navigate to pages that you haven’t tested yet, and promptly complain about them in your FAQ or support section, which is never needed or desired by any party so do yourself and your project a huge favor and test every bit of code (page) that you create.

2. Error Reporting



Testing brings us onto our next important task to check off, and that is error reporting. What do you do when a visitor to your website finds a glitch or a bug inside one of your features, pages or sections? You create a coherent error reporting module that allows visitors to report an error they were experiencing with the website. You can have it route to the support section with the creation of a ticket, or a submit form where the user can explain the issue they were experiencing. Most of the time, these errors are a bug that the developer team did not recognize because the bug reveals itself within the interaction and experience of the end user; labeling the issue as a full-frontend revision required.



1. Finished? Nope! SEO TIME!



Ah yes, the lovely SEO phase. Right when you think you’re finished with a website, you have to keep working harder! ‘Oh joy!’, as Stimpy would say - and all sarcasm aside, it is a truly an important phase of a successful laravel, or any framework built website, for that matter.



So what can you do first to make sure that you are ready to rank on Google? The first thing you must do is make sure you have a ‘robots.txt’ file in your website source folder. This text format file allows Google robots to find your website on their algorithm. Why is this important? Well, it’s not really crucial to have ‘robots’ search your website but moreover, if Google’s robots can find your website pages, then users will have no issue finding it either.



The next most important thing of SEO to achieve is important keywords to place into your website. Find and research exactly the kinds of keywords you want to rank for, assuring your keywords are unique, but not too unique that no one is searching for that sentence - like Goldie Lox - find that perfect middle. Once you do, put them in ‘bold’ on your website and watch your DA and rank go up. Other aspects to keep in mind are making sure your title and website title are matching.
